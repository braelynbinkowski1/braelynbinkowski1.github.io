Recent Experience

2023 Summer Internship at the Van Andel Institute

The Haab Lab specializes in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) and cancer immunodiagnostics. Their most recent work has been directed toward understanding various glycan signatures of cancerous cells to identify and diagnose PDAC. My project focused on the analysis of glycan expression in whole tumor sections within the context of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinomas (PDAC). My primary objective was to identify specific glycan combinations associated with PDAC, and I am excited to report that my research yielded highly promising results and gave the lab an entirely new direction to work in. At the conclusion of my internship, I had the honor of presenting my research findings both orally and through a poster presentation to the institute.

Miami University Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

In the fall semester of 2023, I had the opportunity to serve as an undergraduate teaching assistant for an intro to microbiology lab. With this position, I got hands-on experience with class preparation, planning, and teaching. Working with non-majors and igniting their curiosity was a truly rewarding experience.

2022 Summer Internship at the Van Andel Institute

This internship granted me 10 weeks of intensive laboratory training and work, followed by the opportunity to present to others in the institute. My project in the Haab Lab focused on the effect of glucose and glutamine on the glycan signatures of PDAC cell lines. This project gave the lab much insight concerning the metabolic preferences of glycolytic and lipogenic cell types, which will help us both understand the disease as well as make treatments more specialized and effective. I had the opportunity to present my data in both a PowerPoint presentation as well as poster form. This data has not been published yet but is in the process of being so.

Presentations

Unraveling Pancreatic Cancer Complexity: Investigating Tumor Heterogeneity through Glycan Biomarkers

AUGUST 2023

Numerous glycans are implicated in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), some serving as biomarkers like CA19-9 and sTRA. PDAC patients exhibit varying levels of these biomarkers and other glycans (e.g., Tn antigen, GM2). With this knowledge, I hypothesized a link between glycan expression and tumor subpopulations. Utilizing QuPath for immunofluorescence data analysis, I quantified glycan heterogeneity across entire tumor sections, identifying distinct glycan subpopulations.

The Effect of Glucose and Glutamine Concentration on the Glycosylation of Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma

AUGUST 2022

This study examined metabolite impact on PDAC glycan signature, focusing on glycolytic and lipogenic subtypes. With this research, there is potential to refine PDAC treatment and outcome predictions.
